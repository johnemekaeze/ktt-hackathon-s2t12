## What this project does

This dashboard helps Rwandan community leaders — Abunzi and Umudugudu chiefs — identify households at highest risk of childhood stunting. It uses synthetic NISR-style data to:

1. Score every household with a risk model (logistic regression + rule-based)
2. Display a Folium choropleth map aggregated at the sector level
3. Let district health workers filter by district and risk threshold
4. Export printable A4 "Umudugudu pages" for chiefs without laptops

| File | Rows | Description |
|---|---|---|

| `gold_stunting_flag.csv` | 300 | Labelled subset used to calibrate the logistic model |
| `districts.geojson` | 5 | Simplified polygons for Nyarugenge, Gasabo, Kicukiro, Bugesera, Rulindo |

## Model

**Hybrid: Rule-based + Logistic Regression**

| Feature | Weight (rule-based) |
|---|---|
| Meal frequency deficit | 30% |
| Unsafe water source | 25% |
| Poor sanitation tier | 25% |
| Low income band | 12% |
| Children under 5 | 8% |

The logistic regression is trained on the 300-row gold set and blended (60 LR / 40 rule-based) for stability.

**Metrics on gold set:** Accuracy ≈ 0.82, Precision ≈ 0.80, Recall ≈ 0.83

## Product & Business Adaptation

### The paper delivery channel

Village chiefs in Rwanda often lack laptops or reliable electricity. The dashboard solves this with a **monthly A4 paper loop**:

1. **District health office** runs `python generate_printables.py` on the last Friday of each month
2. Prints one A4 page per sector — 10 high-risk households, anonymised IDs only
3. **Sector coordinator** distributes to village chief (Umudugudu) at the monthly community meeting
4. Chief **annotates** households visited ("done", "escalate", "absent") using a pen
5. Coordinator **collects annotated sheets** and updates the digital tracker
6. **Severe cases** (score > 0.80 + child visible wasting) are escalated to the nearest MINISANTE health post within 48 hours

### Privacy

No names appear on printed pages. Household IDs are positional references (`HH-01`, `HH-02`…) valid only within that report. The ID-to-household mapping is held by the district health office under Rwanda Data Protection Law.

### Cost estimate

| Item | Unit cost | Monthly (per district) |
|---|---|---|
| Printing 5 sector pages × 5 districts | ~200 RWF / page | ~5,000 RWF (~$4.50) |
| Coordinator travel | 2,000 RWF / visit | ~10,000 RWF |

## Local context — Rwanda stunting baselines

Based on NISR / DHS data:
- **National baseline** ~38% stunting prevalence (DHS 2019-20)
- **Nyarugenge** (urban Kigali): ~22% — lower due to better WASH and income
- **Bugesera / Rulindo** (rural): ~40-45% — driven by low sanitation coverage and subsistence farming

This dashboard uses ~22% synthetic base prevalence, consistent with the brief.
